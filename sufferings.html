The Sufferings of Jesus Christ

Agony in the Garden

On the night before His crucifixion, Jesus went to the Garden of Gethsemane to pray. Knowing the suffering that awaited Him, He was deeply distressed and sorrowful (Matthew 26:36-38). He prayed, “Father, if it is possible, let this cup pass from Me. Yet not as I will, but as You will” (Matthew 26:39).

His agony was so intense that His sweat became like drops of blood (Luke 22:44). His disciples, meanwhile, could not stay awake to pray with Him (Matthew 26:40-41).

Betrayal and Arrest

Jesus was betrayed by Judas Iscariot, one of His own disciples. Judas identified Jesus to the soldiers by giving Him a kiss (Matthew 26:47-49). Jesus was arrested, and all His disciples fled (Matthew 26:56).

False Trials and Mockery

Jesus was taken to the high priest Caiaphas, where He was falsely accused by many witnesses (Matthew 26:59-60). He was mocked, beaten, and spat upon (Matthew 26:67-68). He was then sent to Pontius Pilate, the Roman governor, and later to Herod, before returning to Pilate for sentencing (Luke 23:6-11).

The Scourging

Though Pilate found no guilt in Him, he had Jesus scourged to satisfy the crowd (John 19:1). Jesus was whipped brutally with a Roman flagrum, a whip designed to tear flesh. Soldiers then placed a crown of thorns on His head, dressed Him in a purple robe, and mocked Him as “King of the Jews” (John 19:2-3).

Carrying the Cross

Jesus was forced to carry His own cross to the place of crucifixion, known as Golgotha (John 19:17). Weakened by the scourging, He collapsed along the way. Simon of Cyrene was compelled to carry the cross for Him (Luke 23:26).

The Crucifixion

At Golgotha, Jesus was nailed to the cross—hands and feet pierced (Luke 23:33). The soldiers cast lots for His clothing (John 19:23-24). He was crucified between two criminals, and above Him a sign read: “This is Jesus, the King of the Jews” (Matthew 27:37).

As He hung in agony, the crowd mocked Him. “He saved others, but He can’t save Himself!” they said (Matthew 27:42). Jesus prayed, “Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they are doing” (Luke 23:34).

Final Moments

From noon to 3 PM, darkness covered the land (Matthew 27:45). In His final moments, Jesus cried out, “My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?” (Matthew 27:46), and then, “It is finished” (John 19:30). He gave up His spirit and died.

At that moment, the temple curtain tore from top to bottom, the earth shook, and many tombs broke open (Matthew 27:51-52). A Roman centurion declared, “Surely He was the Son of God” (Matthew 27:54).

The Burial

Jesus’ body was taken down and laid in a new tomb owned by Joseph of Arimathea (Matthew 27:57-60). A large stone was rolled in front of the entrance, and guards were posted to secure it (Matthew 27:66).
